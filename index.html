Due to an ever-changing software development environment, the task of choosing a language or framework, and sticking to it, seems increasingly daunting. Every now and then, a new candidate reaches the top, a new state-of-the-art platform becomes the cool kid in the block and not mastering it quick enough feels like you're losing the trendy train. As a fairly veteran dev, I have seen many come and go, that's why I usually stick with the classics. Having said that, I cannot help myself from getting an itch for the new options and, when they are mature enough, diving into them and trying to get the most from what they offer.

The purpose of this portfolio is to showcase the possibilities of development frameworks and tools, some of the lesser known and underused ones, which offer great potential and are oftentimes not even considered on the drawing board. As this page is an evolving site, it may seem that many great options have been ignored. I aim to cover the most common requirements from the users, which are surprisingly not that many if you scrape the surface. In time, I will also cover the more stablished frameworks and compared them with the underdogs.

G Suite

The following project demonstates the power of G Suite which, with a plethora of APIs and tools available, makes for a gentle learning curve in development and an easy deployment process of rich web content. The data displayed on the different reports comes from multiple sources and allows the user to evaluate the conditions in different geographical areas.

Wordpress
